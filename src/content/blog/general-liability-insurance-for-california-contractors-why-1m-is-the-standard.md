## What general liability covers

- **Bodily injury** — if a third party gets hurt because of your work (someone trips on your toolbox, an apprentice drops a hammer on a homeowner's foot).
- **Property damage** — if you damage the property you're working on or adjacent property (water leak from a plumbing job, a paint splash, a hole in a wall).
- **Completed operations** — covers claims after you finish the job (a roof leaks 6 months later because of your work).
- **Personal & advertising injury** — covers claims like libel or copyright infringement in your business activities.

## What GL does NOT cover

GL is general liability — it does NOT cover: damage to your tools or equipment (need Inland Marine), injuries to your own employees (need Workers' Comp), poor workmanship claims unless they cause secondary damage (need Professional Liability), or your vehicles (need Commercial Auto). Most California contractors carry GL plus Workers' Comp plus Commercial Auto bundled into a Business Owner's Policy (BOP).

## What it costs

A small one-person trade in California typically pays $400–$1,200/year for $1M/$2M general liability, depending on trade, gross receipts, and claims history. Roofers, HVAC, and electrical trades are higher risk and price higher than painters, handymen, or landscapers. Annual gross receipts above $250k usually push you to a higher premium tier.
