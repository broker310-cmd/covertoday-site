## What Is SR-22 Insurance?

An SR-22 is not actually a type of insurance — it is a certificate of financial responsibility that your insurance company files with the California DMV on your behalf. It proves that you carry at least the state minimum auto insurance coverage. Think of it as a guarantee from your insurer to the state that you are maintaining valid auto insurance coverage.

## Why Might You Need an SR-22 in California?

California may require you to file an SR-22 if you have been convicted of a DUI or DWI, caught driving without insurance, involved in an at-fault accident without insurance, had your license suspended or revoked, accumulated too many points on your driving record, or received certain serious traffic violations. The California DMV will notify you if an SR-22 filing is required as part of your license reinstatement process.

## How Long Do You Need SR-22 in California?

In California, you are typically required to maintain your SR-22 filing for three years from the date of your conviction or the date your driving privileges are reinstated. It is critical that you maintain continuous coverage during this period — if your policy lapses even for one day, your insurance company is required to notify the DMV, which can result in immediate license suspension.

## How Much Does SR-22 Insurance Cost in California?

The SR-22 filing itself typically costs between $15 and $25. However, the bigger cost impact is on your insurance premium. Because SR-22 is usually required after a serious violation, your insurance rates will likely increase. The amount of the increase depends on the reason for the SR-22, your overall driving history, your age and location, and the insurance carrier.
